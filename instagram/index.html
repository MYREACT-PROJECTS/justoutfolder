The essential oil of Juniperberry is produced from the berries of the Juniperus communis L.; an evergreen shrub up to 6m high native from all northern hemisphere, that can be found from India to North European countries as well as in southern regions of Europe. It has small flowers and little blue berries. The oil is steam distilled from young branches and leaves, containing or not the berries. In some places the oil is steam distilled only from the berries, giving a different product more focused for flavour applications. The Argan oil is extracted from the kernel of the fruit of the Argania spinosa tree; a thorny tree endemic of the arid and semi-arid regions of Morocco and Algeria. The argan tree can grow as high as eight to ten meters and can live up to two hundred years. One tree produces approximately 10kg of fruit per year. The soft pulp removed from the fruit is used as feed for goats and the seeds are then cold pressed for the production of the vegetable oil.
